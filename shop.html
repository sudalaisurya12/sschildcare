Benefits of Minor Millet in Childern

Millet based mid-day meals can boost a child’s growth by 50 per cent in three months, according to a study of 1,500 children in Karnataka which was released by Niti Ayog. (link to the article in the end)

CRISAT scientists who have been part of the research say that improvement in the children was noted in terms of weight and also length. An increase in height was noted in a few subjects as the nutritional intake by the body improved.

In a first ever scientific study of its kind in schools, millet based midday meals were found to significantly boost child growth in terms of body mass index (BMI), nutrition and also can be a better rice substitute in India. Findings of the study published in the journal Nutrients were released on Wednesday by NITI Aayog member Ramesh Chand, and CEO of the National Rainfed Area Authority, Ashok Dalwai.

Benefits of Millets

  • These gluten-free grains are packed with minerals and many vitamins.
  • The alkaline nature of minor millet makes it easy to digest and is a perfect food to suit the soft digestive system of children.
  • These are high in protein and are helpful for muscle growth and development.
  • he smart carbohydrates with fibre can help prevent the risk of obesity in young children.
  • Presence of magnesium can help the formation of bones and teeth.
  • Celiac disease or gluten insensitivity among children can be prevented with minor millet based foods.

Note:

Consult your paediatrician before starting to feed Minor millet based food to your babies.

Start millet based meals for your babies after they are 6 months old.
